Week 14: Studio Work, Portfolio, and Final Exam Study Session - Key Concepts

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this week, you should be able to work independently on your final portfolio project. You should prepare for the final exam. You should complete and document your portfolio work.

Key Concepts

Independent Project Work

This week is dedicated to independent studio work on your final portfolio project. Use this time to refine, complete, and polish your work. Apply all the concepts you've learned throughout the course to create a cohesive, well-executed project.

Focus Areas:

  • Complete your final portfolio project
  • Refine and polish existing work
  • Test and debug your projects
  • Document your creative process
  • Prepare for presentation

Portfolio Preparation

Your portfolio should showcase your best work from the course. Select projects that demonstrate your understanding of key concepts and your creative growth.

Portfolio Checklist:

  • Select your best projects
  • Ensure all projects are working and documented
  • Write project descriptions explaining your creative process
  • Include code comments and documentation
  • Prepare for presentation and discussion

Final Exam Study Session

This week includes a study session to prepare for the final exam. Review key concepts from throughout the course. Focus on understanding how different techniques work together and how to apply them creatively.

Review Topics:

  • Core p5.js concepts (drawing, animation, interaction)
  • Programming fundamentals (variables, functions, loops, conditionals)
  • Data structures (arrays, objects)
  • Advanced techniques (state machines, collision detection, scoring)
  • Video and audio input
  • UI controls and interactive systems

This Week's Deliverables

This week you will complete your portfolio project and prepare for the final exam study session.

Tips for Success

Use this studio time effectively. Focus on completing and refining your work. Review course materials and examples. Test your projects thoroughly. Document your creative process. Prepare questions for your instructor if needed. Take breaks and manage your time well.
